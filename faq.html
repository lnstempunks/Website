Frequently Asked Questions

1. Who are we?

We are the L&N STEMpunks! Founded in 2011, the same year as the L&N STEM Academy, our team began work towards achieving the FIRST mission and goals. With our school being brand new, there were only freshmen and sophomores. We spent the first off season building a robot for Knoxville’s annual Veterans’ Day parade. The robot was simple since it’s main function was saluting. Sadly, our competition robot was not complete come the bag-n-tag day during our first season. But we spent the majority of our competition finishing/fixing our robot, so by the end of the week we had a robot that could pick up and shoot the foam basketballs.

In our second year, the team doubled in size, along with the addition of juniors to our team. In order to manage the extra members, our team started having student leadership. To continue in our efforts to spread the FIRST message, our team once again participated in the Veterans’ Day parade, this time showing off our Rebound Rumble robot. The year 2013 was the first season we had a real engineering schedule. We formed a Gantt chart and followed the schedule as closely as possible. Our robot was put in the bag with a complete, working chassis, a Frisbee shooter, and a pyramid climber. Once again we had a busy competition because we were working constantly to finish the robot, but we were able to practice with it on the practice field.

2. Where are we?

Our school, the L&N STEM Academy, started in 2011 as the first STEM magnet school in Knoxville, Tennessee. Our school is designed to focus on STEM (an acronym for Science, Technology, Engineering and Math) education. We have a STEM class that all students take from Freshman to Senior year (STEM 1-4) and end with an actual internship in a student's desired field.

3. What about sponsoring/donations?

If you wish you sponsor us please email us at stempunks@outlook.com. If you wish to donate to us you can view the Dontations tab on the Home Page. In 2013-2014 we raised for a sign restoration for our school, people wanted to know how much went to the sign and how much went to us. Approximately $6500 went to the sign and the rest of the donations (if not specified to the sign restore) were accept by us with a smile.
