MEET THE TEAM

The Team

We're students from the L&N STEM Academy with a passion to learn and innovate. We participate in the regional FIRST Robotics competition every year. Although the FIRST competition is about building a robot, many of our members specialize in other fields such as design, business management, media production, and programming.

COACHES AND MENTORS

Karen Cheng

Karen Cheng is an East TN native and grew up in the Secret City of Oak Ridge. After receiving her bachelors from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign, Ms. Cheng returned to Knoxville and worked as a research associate at the Center for Environmental Biotechnology at UT-Knoxville. During her research and mentoring of undergrads, she realized her true love was in educational outreach and sharing her passion for math, science, and technology with students. She completed her Masters in Secondary Education at UT and began her exciting adventures at the L&N STEM Academy in Fall 2012.

Coach since 2013

Tim Klein

Tim Klein graduated from The University of Tennessee with a B.A. in Anthropology in 1993 and has worked in the Information Technology and Business Intelligence field since 1996. He has been building his own hobby robots since 2001 and enjoys sharing the challenge with kids of all ages.

Programming Mentor since 2011

Sebastian Acosta-Nijamkin

Sebastian Acosta-Nijamkin, Sebas in the FRC world, has been involved in FIRST since 2004. He started out as a student on team 1251, TechTigers, from Coconut Creek, FL. After multiple championships and trips to Nationals, back then in Atlanta, he spent two years as a mentor for 1251. Now in Knoxville, Mr. Acosta, is excited about working with the STEMpunks and seeing this great young team grow.

Assistant Coach since 2013

Randy Bueckman

In the 36+ years of working in Biomedical Engineering, Randy Bueckman has transitioned from vacuum tubes through transistors and integrated circuits to surface mount technology. In the start of his career, individual computers were housed in rooms – not your lap. His first home-made computer utilized an 8080 micro-processor using wire wrap connectors and was little more than a calculator with some IO ports. He has always had great interest in automation. His sons have been interested in robots for years. Through FIRST® Robotics and the L&N STEMpunks team they can work on them together!

Mentor since 2011
