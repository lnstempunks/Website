About Us

TEAM MENTORS ROBOTS OUTREACH SCHOOL

The Team

We're students from the L&N STEM Academy with a passion to learn and innovate. We participate in the regional FIRST Robotics Competition every year. Although the FIRST competition is about building a robot, many of our members specialize in other fields such as design, business management, media production, and programming.

TEAM CAPTAIN

Henry Gertsen

Henry Gertsen is a senior at the L&N STEM Academy. A four year member of the team, he has been with the L&N STEMpunks since the L&N STEM Academy was opened. Henry's leadership helps keep the L&N STEMpunks on track and engaged in the community.

henry@lnstempunks.org

PROMOTIONAL

Collateral

The Collateral Team ideates, designs, and creates our team's physical branding standards. This includes our team's shirts, costumes, competition pit, and parade floats.

Lead by Alyssa Cox-Lewis

Communications

The Communications Team manages the team's public identity. With the top Facebook, Twitter, and Instagram pages in the state, our communications team has successfully built one of the strongest social media programs in the FIRST Robotics Competition. The communications team also develops entries for the Safety Animation Award and Chairman's Award, having won third in the world in 2014's Safety Animation Award. They also work on developing this website and documenting the build season through pictures and video.

Lead by Josh Witt

BUSINESS

Finance

Our Finance Team is responsible for reaching out to local businesses to seek their support, either financially or otherwise.

Lead by Kishan Tailor

Human Resources

Our Human Resources team is responsible for reaching out to local schools, daycares, and other programs to get the team involved in the community. HR is also responsible for training and supporting new members of the team and introducing them to the FIRST program.

Lead by Daniel Bueckman

ENGINEERING

Mechanical

The Mechanical Team is responsible for designing the functional elements of our team's robots. Mechanical goes through extensive ideation and prototyping before finally coming to a final design for each year's FRC robot.

Lead by Nate Heinrich

Electrical

The Electrical Team creates the systems that power and move the robot. Electrical rigorously ensures that every aspect of the robot works to it's full potential.

Lead by Jonathan Bueckman

Programming

The Programming Team uses the Python programming language to develop innovative autonomous programs and control systems for our robots. Programming tirelessly bugfixes and optimizes code up until the day of competition.

Lead by Ben Klein

SCHOOL

Our team is based at the L&N STEM Academy in downtown Knoxville, Tennessee.

L&N STEM Academy

The L&N STEM Academy was founded in 2011 as a magnet high school focused on the disciplines of science, technology, engineering, and math in the Knox County School system. The school began with Freshman and Sophomore classes, expanding each year to have the first graduating class in 2013-14. The academic program is based on full-year classes, where the maximum course load is 8 simultaneous credits. An integrated curriculum and project-based instruction is made possible by the 1:1 technology implemented throughout the academy.

PRINCIPAL

Becky Ashe

Becky Ashe has been the principal of the L&N STEM Academy since it's inception in 2011. Under her guidance, the L&N STEM Academy has grown from under 200 students to 600 from multiple East Tennessee counties.

MAKER SPACE

A shared school space, the Maker Space serves as our robotics lab and an area for students to come and build anything they imagine. The Maker Space includes a band saw, chop saw, sander, drill press, and three MakerBot Replicator 3D printers.
